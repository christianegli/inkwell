Inkwell

A novel writing platform — guide

Getting started

Just start writing

Open Inkwell and the page is blank. No setup, no wizard. Write your first sentence. Inkwell builds around what you write — not the other way around.

Scenes

Hover the left edge of the screen (or ⌘⇧B) to open the scene panel. Your manuscript is a list of scenes — flat, draggable, no forced hierarchy. Click any scene to switch to it. Click the status dot to cycle: unwritten → drafting → drafted → done. Total word count at the bottom.

Notes

Hover the right edge (or ⌘⇧N) for the scratchpad. Type a note, press Enter to save. Tag with #character #place #idea #todo. Filter by tag. Pin notes to the current scene.

World — characters, places, themes

⌘⇧W opens the World panel — characters, places, themes, timeline. All inline editing. If you've configured an AI key, characters detected in your writing appear here automatically with a confidence badge.

Outline

⌘⇧O switches to a card grid of all your scenes — preview, status, word count. Drag to reorder. Click to go write.

AI — characters, suggestions, analysis

Connect your API key

Open Settings (⌘,) → AI tab. Paste a key for Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Grok. Stored in your browser only — never transmitted except to the respective API.

Auto-extraction

After each save, Inkwell silently scans your manuscript for character names, places, and recurring themes. If it finds something new, a gentle notification appears: "✦ Found 3 characters — tap to review." You accept, edit, or dismiss each suggestion.

On-demand tools (⌘K)

Open the command palette and search for:

Continue sceneGenerates the next paragraph in your style
What happens next?3 scene ideas based on current scene
Tone checkDescribes the emotional register of the scene
Find inconsistenciesScans all scenes for continuity issues
Describe characterWrites a sketch from what's in the manuscript

AI assists. It never rewrites without you asking.

GitHub sync

Save your manuscript to GitHub

Settings (⌘,) → GitHub tab. Enter your Personal Access Token (create at github.com/settings/tokens/new — tick repo), repository, and file path. ⌘⇧S pushes a full JSON snapshot of your manuscript to GitHub. Free version history.
